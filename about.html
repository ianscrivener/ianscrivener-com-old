About Me

hi, my name is Ian Scrivener. I'm an Australian web technology specifialist, fullstack/backend web developer & cloud sysadmin. I've extensive (20 years) experience with medium to large scale web projects for .com, .gov & .org.

My main weapons include javascript (node.js, angular.js, and d3.js), SQL & noSQL databases (mySQL, PostgresSQL, MSSQL, MongoDB, CouchDB etc)

While I don't do 'frontend' web design, I know ugly from pretty & I'm real handy with CSS3, LESS,and SASS. As for UX/IA I'd say I'm midweight, though I can churn out Balsamiq wireframes like I'm on crack and happy to work closely with the business & marketing people facilitating great UX/IA outcomes.

Agile project methodologies/philosophy (including scrum) are preferred - because they are way more efficient, less cumbersome and plain old fun. Though some organisations are more suited to traditional approaches such as Waterfall. I can't imagine not starting a new a project with Trello and GitHub... it would be like eating a boiled egg without salt.

Sysadmin is something I've been doing for 25 years (since back when a x286 was high end and had a maths co-processor). Of course web hosting its all cloud nowadays and still some virtual machines (VMWare). I'm good with both Windows & Linux (Ubuntu) on Rackspace & Amazon, as well as Heroku, Nodejitsu, Digital Ocean, AppFog etc.

Some devops, including Puppet (some Chef), Vagrant - though I haven't done any massive scale projects, usually less than ten servers all up. Really keen to play more with Docker - even on a Raspberry Pi?!

One of my favourite project domains is advanced web mapping and GIS. No not Google Maps! I've done over a dozen high end web mapping applications for a range of government agencies (National Park, Forestry, Fisheries etc). I'm firmly in the free & OpenSource camp; geoserver, openlayers, leaflet

Recently I have started working with d3.js for interactive maps, charts & infographics (see portfolio section) - I'm really loving it! A great blend of pretty design/UX with complex coding behind it all.
